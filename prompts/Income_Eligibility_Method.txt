-   Scenario: Growth/Decline in EBITDA in current year vis-à-vis
> previous year

a.  Where current year's EBITDA % growth is up to 50% of the
> previous year's EBITDA, current year EBITDA is to be
> considered

b.  Where current year's EBITDA % growth is more than 50% of the
> last year's EBITDA, average of last 2 years or 50% growth over
> previous year's EBITDA whichever is higher is to be
> considered.

c.  Where % decline in EBITDA is more than 20% for reasons other
> than business expansion related cost increase, RCC approval is
> required to process the case.

> loss/profit on sale of asset i.e. capital gains, loss on theft,
> speculation income, one time interest, donation etc.) to be
> nullified for computation of Eligible Income

-   EBITDA data is to be taken from the latest financials as filed with
> the Income Tax body/ Audited Reports.

## [Step Up]{.underline}

-   A step-up loan allows customer to pay a lower Equated Monthly
Installment (EMI) during the first few years of repayment and
gradually increase the EMI as the loan term progresses.

-   Enables Customers to purchase their dream home seeking higher Loan
Eligibility basis expected growth in income with lower EMI for
initial 3 years & Higher EMI for balance tenure

-   Product parameters are defined as below:
